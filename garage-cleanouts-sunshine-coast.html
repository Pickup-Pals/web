Common Garage Cleanout Scenarios

Every garage cleanout job we do is different, but there are a few situations that come up time and time again on the Sunshine Coast:

  • Years of accumulated stuff — the slow build-up of things you meant to deal with but never did. Boxes, old tools, furniture that didn't make it inside, sporting equipment the kids have outgrown.
  • Preparing to sell a property — buyers and agents want to see a clean, empty garage. We help vendors get there quickly and without fuss.
  • Deceased estate clearances — a sensitive situation that we handle with care. We work with family members, executors or real estate agents to get the property ready.
  • Post-renovation clean-ups — offcuts, old materials, broken fixtures and general builder's rubbish that needs to go before you can enjoy the finished space.

What We Take in a Garage Cleanout

The short answer: almost everything. Common items we remove in garage cleanouts include:

  • Old tools, toolboxes and workshop equipment
  • Furniture — couches, chairs, shelving units, bed frames
  • Boxes and bags of miscellaneous household items
  • Sporting equipment — bikes, kayaks, gym gear, golf clubs
  • Whitegoods — fridges, washing machines, dryers, dishwashers
  • Tyres (including car, bike and trailer tyres)
  • Broken appliances and electronics
  • Timber, offcuts and general construction materials

What We Can't Take

There are a few things that require specialist disposal and that we're unable to load onto our truck. These include chemicals, paint tins, fuel, solvents, asbestos-containing materials and other hazardous substances. If your garage has any of these items, we can advise on the right disposal options, but we won't be able to take them ourselves.

How It Works

Getting your garage cleaned out with Pickup Pals is straightforward. Here's the process:

  • Get a quote — call us or fill in our online form. Tell us roughly how much needs to go and we'll give you an upfront price.
  • We arrive — our two-person team shows up at the agreed time, ready to work. Same-day availability is often possible.
  • We load everything — you just point at what's going. We carry it all out, load the truck and sweep the floor on the way out.
  • Pay on completion — once the garage is clear and you're happy, you pay. Card or cash accepted. No payment upfront.

Pricing Transparency

Garage cleanout pricing is based on a service fee for our time and the size of the job, plus the actual tip or recycling fees passed on at cost — no markup. We'll quote you clearly before we start, so there are no nasty surprises at the end. For most single-car garages, the job can be done in under two hours. Double garages with heavy accumulation obviously take longer, but we'll always be upfront about timing and cost.
